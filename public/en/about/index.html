Hieneo: tech, code, và cách nhìn của Hiển.

That is the line I want readers to remember. Hieneo is where I write about technology and programming. The name blends Hiển with Neo from The Matrix—a small nod to the technical world, to code and systems, and how I tend to picture them.

About me

On this site I write as “mình” (Vietnamese for “I/me,” in a direct, conversational tone) in the Vietnamese posts; in English I will simply say I.

I work as an iOS developer. Day to day I maintain and extend features for products on Apple’s platform.

In high school, when it was time to pick a direction, I realized I loved playing games—and that nudged me toward information technology. A slightly naive reason at first, but it opened the door to code, and I have kept walking.

What I like to put into a post

What makes me want to write is personal experience, sharing how I see things, and sometimes open questions for readers—an invitation to think and respond.

Why this blog?

I started it to share what I am learning and building, to learn—writing helps me think more clearly and remember longer—and for community: I hope to exchange ideas and get feedback from people who care about tech and code.

Who is it for?

I am writing for developers, friends I want to talk frankly with about technology, and end users of technology—people who may not write code but still want to understand more of what is “under the hood” or how tech shapes daily life.

Language

The site is primarily in Vietnamese. Where it helps (terms, original docs, code), I may mix in English for technical clarity. You can switch languages using the theme’s language selector; the default site language is Vietnamese.

Topics over time

I have not locked a few fixed “pillars” into the blog’s name—the frame stays technology and programming in general, and the themes I want to keep long term will emerge as I publish. iOS and what I do at work will show up often.
